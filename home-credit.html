The problem

Rejected applicants aren't always bad borrowers

Home Credit serves customers often underserved by traditional banks — people with thin or no credit history. Standard models trained only on approved applicants suffer from selection bias: rejected applicants are never observed, so the model never learns from them.

This leads to systematic False Rejection — good borrowers being denied credit simply because they don't fit the profile of past approvals.

A second problem: models trained on resampled data produce systematically overestimated PD values, making them unreliable for IFRS 9 ECL calculation where accurate probability estimates directly affect provisioning amounts.

Approach

9-phase pipeline — from raw data to IFRS 9-ready model

01
Leakage Audit
Identified and removed target-leaking features before any modeling.
02
Anti-Leakage Preprocessing
Feature engineering pipeline designed to avoid data leakage at every transformation step.
03
Reject Inference via Label Propagation
Extended training data with unlabeled rejected applicants using Label Propagation — correcting selection bias by inferring probable outcomes for the unobserved population.
04
Modeling — XGBoost
Trained XGBoost classifier on the bias-corrected dataset with hyperparameter tuning via cross-validation.
05
Regulatory Evaluation
Evaluated model against OJK standards — AUC, KS Statistic, PSI, and Gini coefficient. KS 0.4026 classified as Excellent.
06
Isotonic PD Calibration
Applied CalibratedClassifierCV with isotonic regression to correct systematic PD overestimation. Improved Brier Score by ~55%.
07
IFRS 9 ECL Framing
Calibrated PD feeds into ECL = PD × LGD × EAD, enabling Stage 1/2/3 classification.
08
Threshold Tuning
Optimized approval threshold on validation set to minimize False Rejection Rate while maintaining business targets.
09
SHAP Explanation
Generated global and local SHAP explanations — making the model interpretable for regulators and business stakeholders.

Challenges & solutions

What made this hard

Challenge

Selection bias — model only trained on approved applicants, never seeing the rejected population.

Solution

Reject Inference via Label Propagation — extended training set with inferred labels for rejected applicants.

Challenge

Resampling destroys the class base rate, causing systematic PD overestimation that propagates into ECL calculations.

Solution

Isotonic calibration (CalibratedClassifierCV) applied post-training to realign predicted probabilities with true observed default rates.

Challenge

AUC alone doesn't measure calibration quality — a model can have high AUC while PD estimates are systematically wrong.

Solution

Used Brier Score as calibration quality metric alongside AUC — tracking discrimination and probability accuracy separately.

Key learnings

What this project taught me

PIT vs TTC is determined by the presence or absence of macroeconomic variables — not by the time span of training data. This model is pseudo-TTC (static, no MEV).

A high AUC model can still produce unreliable ECL estimates if PD is not calibrated. Calibration and discrimination are separate quality dimensions.

Reject Inference is not optional in credit risk — ignoring rejected applicants creates a biased model that systematically underestimates risk for certain segments.

Regulatory evaluation and IFRS 9 framing should be designed into the pipeline from the start, not added as an afterthought.
